How does the AI Incubator work?

The AI Incubator runs in groups to create a shared rhythm and strong peer support:

  • A new group starts roughly every 6 months
  • Each group stays in the incubator for a maximum of 9 months
  • The program has three phases:
    • Problem validation (1 month)
    • Solution validation (2 months)
    • Toward your first customer (6 months)

On top of that, teams commit to a "build in public" mindset: each cohort shares its progress openly, along with key lessons and exciting pivots. You're not expected to reveal trade secrets. Public progress keeps focus sharp and makes the journey visible to customers and partners too.

Program phases

1. Problem validation (1 month)

The first month is all about validating the customer problem:

  • Find out whether your target customers have a real, recurring, meaningful problem
  • Confirm that the problem is important enough that customers are willing to pay to solve it
  • Identify who is affected most by the problem

If the problem doesn't validate, you have two paths: restart Phase 1 with a new angle or a new problemâ€”or leave the program. This saves you months of building the wrong thing.

2. Solution validation (2 months)

In this phase, you build a nearly working prototype to test how well your solution actually solves the customer's problem.

During this phase:

  • You create a prototype that customers can genuinely try
  • You test whether your product solves the problem in a way customers truly care about

If the solution doesn't validate, the team can:

  • Return to Phase 1,
  • Repeat Phase 2,
  • Or leave the program.

Validation matters more than a slick demo.

3. Toward your first customer (6 months)

The final phase is real startup life.

In this phase you'll:

Moving forward is a condition for staying in the program. If a team can't make progress, they will have to leave the incubator.

  • You have up to 6 months to release your first version
