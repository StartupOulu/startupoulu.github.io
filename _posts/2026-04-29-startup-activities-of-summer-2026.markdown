Summer 2026 has been one of the most active seasons in the history of the Finnish startup ecosystem. From Oulu to Helsinki, founders gathered at events, accelerators ran full cohorts, and new companies raised their first rounds. The energy was palpable — and the numbers backed it up.

The season kicked off in June with the StartupOulu Summer Summit, which drew over 400 attendees from across Northern Finland. Investors from Stockholm and Berlin made the trip north, drawn by a growing reputation for deep tech and industrial innovation. Several promising seed deals were signed on the sidelines of the event.

July saw a wave of accelerator demo days. Three cohorts — spanning health tech, clean energy, and SaaS — presented to packed audiences in Oulu and Tampere. Of the fifteen teams that took the stage, eight had already secured pilot customers by the time they pitched. Judges noted the unusually high level of commercial traction for early-stage companies.

August brought the summer's biggest funding news. Two Oulu-based startups closed rounds exceeding one million euros each, while a third announced a strategic partnership with a major European industrial group. The announcements drew coverage from Finnish and Scandinavian tech media, putting the region on the radar of a broader investor audience.

As autumn approaches, the momentum shows no sign of slowing. New cohort applications are open, several companies are preparing follow-on raises, and the community calendar is already filling up. Summer 2026 will be remembered as the season the Oulu startup scene grew up — and set its sights firmly on Europe.
